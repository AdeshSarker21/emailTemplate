Like all Medicare Supplements, the PLAN G and PLAN N are federally regulated and standardized which means the benefits are required to be the same with every insurance company that offers them.  Most Medicare recipients have been selecting the PLAN G over the last couple of years because it offers the most comprehensive coverage. Plan G covers 100% of all Medicare approved expenses except for the small annual deductible of $233. Once you have satisfied the $233 deductible, EVERYTHING is covered 100% for the rest of the year.

Excess charges are not common and can be avoided because statistically 95% of Doctor’s accept Medicare Assignment.  You can also avoid by simply asking your doctor if they accept Medicare assignment or charge excess charges.

Below is a brief description of the 4 major components of Medicare: Part A is (HOSPITAL INSURANCE)

Part A is (HOSPITAL INSURANCE)

Medicare Part A covers inpatient hospital, skilled nursing facility, and some home health care services. 
The Medicare Part A inpatient deductible will be $1,556 in 2022 if ever admitted to the hospital. A Medicare Supplement will cover the Part A deductible.

Part B is (MEDICAL INSURANCE)

Medicare Part B covers physician services, outpatient hospital services, certain home health services, durable medical equipment, and certain other medical and health services not covered by Medicare Part A.  For most people Part B will cost $170.10 a month.  It will be deducted out of your Social Security check if you are drawing Social Security.  If you are not drawing Social Security, you will be billed quarterly. There is a small annual deductible for Part B which is only $233 for 2022. Once the deductible has been met, Medicare will pay only 80%, you will be responsible for the other 20%. A Medicare Supplement will cover the 20% that Medicare Part B approved will not pay.

Part C- Medicare Advantage

Medicare Solutions is certified to offer these plans and we can do so if desired.  However, there are some limitations to this type of plan which needs to be clearly understood. THESE PLANS SHOULD ONLY BE CONSIDERED IF YOU CANNOT AFFORD THE PREMIUM FOR A MEDICARE SUPPLEMENT.  The premiums could be slightly less when compared to a Supplement, however they do come with a lot of unpredictable costs with high Copays and deductibles. Most people DO NOT realize that these plans replace Original Medicare as your

your Primary Insurance. Unlike a Medicare Supplement, a Medicare Advantage Plan replaces your Original Medicare. Your health coverage is taken over completely by the insurance company and you do not have the freedom to simply go to any doctor that accepts Medicare. You are subject to the plan networks, and rules for referrals and services. In most cases, if you go to a doctor that takes Original Medicare but does not take your Medicare Advantage Plan you will have to pay full cost for the service. Some Medicare Advantage Plans do have Preferred Provider PPO Networks that allow you to go out of Network, however, the doctor you see does not have to accept your plans negotiated rates and you may have to pay out of pocket and get some portion reimbursed from the plan. You will still be required to pay your Part B monthly premium of $170.10. Although the plans may be offered for free or a low monthly premium by the private insurance company, you will still be subject to co-pays, deductibles and high out of pocket expenses. A lot of these plans have an annual spending limit that can reach as high as $6,900.
